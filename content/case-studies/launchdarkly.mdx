## A reliable maverick.

<p className="lead">In 2019, I reached out to LaunchDarkly for an open Senior Brand Designer role. Ghosted. No one ever replied to my email.</p>

Just a year later, in 2020, I was at Focus Lab when a client rolled into the qualified leads funnel. It was LaunchDarkly needing an end-to-end rebrand. Enter: me.

LaunchDarkly was great to work with. Keith Messik, SVP Marketing, and Dustin Haver, Head of Design, were fantastic partners in bringing the new identity to life.

## Halftone, halftone, halftone.

<p className="lead">The visual language grew from a place of outer space — grayscale, with occasional blips of color. Photography was halftoned to amplify a brand rooted in futurism, while allowing other elements to balance a tempered nostalgia.</p>

We used halftone patterns, open-source imagery of planets and stars, and primary shapes set in unique brand colors to establish an iconic visual ecosystem.

### Typography

<p className="lead">What a joy to pair Audimat 3000 (Jack Usine) with Söhne (Klim). Their partnership plays an essential part of the identity. It lets LaunchDarkly be boast-worthy while remaining reliable and assured.</p>

Audimat 3000 carries a subtle blend of rigidity and eccentricity. Söhne is more no-nonsense — its DNA in Akzidenz-Grotesk — making it a suitable option for longer-form text where Audimat catches the eye.
